Our Functions

Empowering Zambia's Democracy

The Electoral Commission of Zambia provides essential services to ensure free, fair, and credible elections nationwide. Explore our core functions that empower citizens and strengthen democracy.

Delimitation

Delimitation is the process of mapping and re-aligning of electoral boundaries.

Voter Registration

The Electoral Act empowers the commission to carry out continuous voters registration.

Voter Education

The Electoral Process Act No. 35 of 2016 Section 80 states that the Commission shall provide Voter Education for an election.

Elections

Formal process by which registered voters cast their ballot to choose an individual to hold office of the President, Mayor, Council Chairperson, Member of National Assembly or Councillor.

Conflict Management

One of the mandates of the Electoral Commission of Zambia is to manage electoral conflicts and resolve disputes, as set out in Section 113 of the Electoral Process Act No.35 2016.

Accreditation

Elections in Zambia are open to observation and monitoring by international and local organisations respectively.

ECZ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about the Electoral Commission of Zambia’s mandates and processes.

What is Delimitation?

Delimitation is the process of mapping and re-aligning electoral boundaries to ensure fair representation of constituencies, wards, and polling districts.

How does Voter Registration work?

The Electoral Act empowers ECZ to conduct continuous voter registration so that eligible citizens can register to vote or update their details at any time.

What is Voter Education?

According to Section 80 of the Electoral Process Act No. 35 of 2016, ECZ is mandated to provide voter education to ensure that citizens are well-informed about the electoral process.

What elections does ECZ conduct?

ECZ oversees the formal process by which registered voters cast their ballot to choose individuals for positions such as President, Member of Parliament, Mayor, Council Chairperson, and Councillor.
